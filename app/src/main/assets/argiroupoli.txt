The Village of Argiroupoli is located on the slopes of the Lefka Ori, surrounded by plane trees and chestnut trees, it is known for its springs and its waterfalls. It stands on the ruins of Lappa which was among the most important Roman cities of western Crete. In the village there are the chapel of the five Virgins, the remains of Roman baths, the ruins of the ancient temple dedicated to Poseidon, Venetian columns and an ancient Roman Gate, as well as the churches of Agios Ioannis and Santa Paraskev?, especially known for the mosaic floor of the first century BC with about 7000 pieces.
