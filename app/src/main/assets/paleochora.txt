Is a small town south of Crete, which became popular in the 70s with hippies and developed for tourism thanks to its crystal clear sea and well organized beaches, all different from each other. Its name means "old earth" and according to some researchers would be built on the ruins of the ancient city of Kalamydi. Venetians, pirates and Ottomans have left their traces. Do not miss to visit the ruins of the castle Selino, built in 1278, the churches Anidroi and Voutas with Venetian and Byzantine frescoes, the city museum and the beautiful beaches of Pachia Ammos, Chalikia, Grammeno, Gialiskari and Anidroi. They are accessible by car and are equipped for tourists with parking, toilets, bars, taverns, sun loungers and parasols.
