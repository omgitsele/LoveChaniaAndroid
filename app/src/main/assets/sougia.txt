It is located 60 km from the city of Chania, south of the island. Flourished during the Roman and Byzantine Empire, was a port of the city Dorica di Elyros destroyed by the Saracens in the ninth century AD. Sougia today is a small village by the sea, away from mass tourism and offers a tranquility that attracts all those in search of rest. Its huge wide pebble beach with its crystal clear and deep waters, is fascinating. Accessible by car, bus and boat from Agia Roumeli and Paleochora, Sougia is equipped for tourists and has parking, restrooms, bars, taverns, sun loungers and parasols. A party is also frequented by nudists.
