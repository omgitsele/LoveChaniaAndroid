A historical museum worth visiting in Platanias which tells the sufferings of the Greek people during the Second World War while the German occupation. The caves of Platanias were dug by the inhabitants as refuge from the bombing and are now a memorial museum. Its village was a strategic position and was during the occupation the seat of the barracks and shelters for the ammunition. The museum caves, dug under the church of Agios Dimitris, are 120 meters long and have 10 rooms without windows and two exits that lead into the trenches. After years of neglect, limestone rock has favored their perfect preservation, and today you can see vehicles, weapons and ammunition as well as photographs of time.
